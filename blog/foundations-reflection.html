Foundations reflection

I never would have thought that a software development course would put so much emphasis on human skills. I thought software developers were nerds working in a basement somewhere. Oh how I was wrong...

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I can honestly say I haven't learnt much during the Human Skills aspect. Understanding this was something I needed in the military to become an effective leader. The military puts a lot of emphasis on human skills including self-realisation and growth mindset during their leadership training.

One thing that I learnt was telling myself to "get better thoughts" when I'm feeling anxious or frustrated. This simple act allows me to focus on being nice to myself by telling myself the situation is better than I'm thinking.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values: tend to be the things that drive an individual. These are typically unwavering or non-negotiable. When working with others it helps to understand these others values so that you may shape your intentions to match their values.

Empathy: is your ability to understand what others are feeling. Understanding this allows you to shape your intent to enable others to feel valued and increase their productivity.

Self-awareness: Is your own ability to know who you are. This is a hugely important aspect. Without knowing your weaknesses you limit your own growth. Understanding you weaknesses enables you to create prompts to address them when required and allow you to turn them towards your strengths.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

The thing that surprised me the most was how much emphasis is placed on the Human Skills aspect on this course. I always thought software developers were reclusive individuals so it was surprising to find out that is definitely not the case. But it also makes a lot of sense considering most projects require small teams to work together to create them

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

I think that for each individual that conducts any type of self reflection it can be very confronting and I understand that some people would want to shy away from this. But at some point we have to realise that we are only confronting ourselves. And if we can't survive through our own self-criticism them how are we going to accept criticism from others in order to allow growth

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe this is a focus for a number of reasons. Mainly:

  • 1. Learning programming is like learning a new language and can be quite a hard task. Human skills helps by promoting a growth mindset which increases the likelihood that students will pass the course.
  • 2. Programmers work in teams to create. So we must learn how to work in a team. Furthermore we will likely need to work with other teams. Employers will be looking for people who can play nicely with others.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer

I think it's awesome that there is an emphasis on human skills. Everyone should learn some form of human skills for their own personal growth despite the industry you intend to work in
