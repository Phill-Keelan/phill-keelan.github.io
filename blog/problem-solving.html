Problem Solving

My brain craves stimulation! If events aren't meaningful or challenging it is very hard for me to stay focused. I find myself getting bored and allowing myself to become distracted. For this reason my brain really enjoys finding patterns and solving problems

Blocked on a Simple Problem

During the JavaScript Kata 2: Manipulating objects challenge I learnt a lot of basic syntax. In my head I was thinking it was going to get more and more difficult so when I got down to the final question (question 11) I figured it was gonna be super complicated. I started writing out this elaborate code to pull properties from one object and place it in another. But I just could not get it to work. So I stood up and walked away. Pouring over some old notes later on I found the answer to question could be so simple but I was in disbelief that it would work. Decided to try it anyway...lo and behold it worked...face palm...lol

I tried to google the solution but my prompts were too elaborate trying to explain to much to google. I tried the rubber ducky method by talking it through with my dog. I tried copilot but I didn't understand what it was trying to tell me. I felt myself getting heated so it was time for a break and I decided to get up and clear my head. Through this experience I learnt to keep it simple.

Elegantly Solved a Problem

I was a little lost when I first read question 6 of the Gradebook Challenge. I decided to take a step back and change my approach. Up until this point I had been googling a lot and using co-pilot to explain things to me. This time I decided to write out everything in a hierarchal fashion on a notepad so that I could understand where everything was. I was then able to clearly see how the syntax should be written.
