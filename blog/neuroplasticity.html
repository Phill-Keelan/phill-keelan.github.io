Neurolasticity and Growth Mindset

Benefits of Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is defined as the ability of the nervous system to change its activity in response to intrinsic or extrinsic stimuli by reorganizing its structure, functions, or connections after injuries, such as a stroke or traumatic brain injury (TBI)

Although the above is the definition, according to the US National Institute of Health, it isn't limited to recovery from brain injuries. Understanding that the brain has this ability is the key to unlocking your learning potential.

The basic idea is that with proper training we can develop new neurological pathways. More that that is the more you use these neural pathways the more they become the default path. This can be illustrated much like a path through the woods, the more you walk it the more you walk it the more definitive the path will become.

Increase Neuroplasticity

What is Growth Mindset

A Growth Mindset fit hand in hand with the idea of neuroplasticity. A growth mindset is about approaching every challenge and every failure as an opportunity for learning. This is opposite to the Fixed Mindset in which a person thinks that talent and knowledge are fixed attributes. Understanding and employing a growth mindset leads to exponential growth

Yes Man

An interesting movie that highlights a growth mindset, unbeknown to most people who have watched it, is "Yes Man". This quirky comedy starring funny man Jim Carrey sees the main character enter the movie saying no to everything and doesn't even tr. Until one day he attends a seminar in which he feels he is singled out and has to say yes to everything thereafter or something bad will happen. Fast forward 10 or so minutes and some events solidify the belief that he will be in peril if he says no to anything. And so the he starts saying yes. Low and behold his life improves.

The idea is that when he says no to everything in the beginning he is displaying a closed mindset. He thinks his life is what it is and has stopped trying. After saying yes to everything he starts learning new things and experiencing new experiences. All these new activities increase his confidence and he is less apprehensive to give the next activity ago. He has ow shifted to having a growth mindset.
