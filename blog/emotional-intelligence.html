Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence is a persons ability to identify, understand and manage situations involving there own and/or someone else's emotions and how they will effect the situation.

Emotional Quotient (EQ) vs Intelligence Quotient (IQ)

IQ is your ability to understand the problem as it is. EQ is your ability to understand how solving the problem will affect those nvolved.Let's simplify a well known conflict, the Battle of Troy. In this story Paris, son of the Trojan King, ran off with Helen, the wife of Menelaus whose brother was the great commander Agamemnon. Imagine ourself as the King/Queen of Troy

Problem: Youngest son returns home with another man's wife. Her husband's brother will bring an army to fetch her.

IQ Answer: Return the girl and let Paris be killed by the hand of Menelaus. Prevent a war that will soak up 10 years of energy and resources.

EQ Response: Paris will be dead and Helen will be miserable for the rest of her days. You, as the king/queen will need to bury and grieve over your son's death. Civil unrest in the kingdom due to the people believing you gave up your son to protect yourselves.

Q Response: We need to protect our loved ones. The walls will hold off the greek attacks.

Outcome: To protect the ones you love you decide to fight confident that you can defend against the Greek forces.

Why is EQ important?

It is important to understand EQ so that you may thrive in society through understanding all elements external to the physical. You can appreciate you're own thoughts and feelings as well as those of others whether they be close friends or passersby.
